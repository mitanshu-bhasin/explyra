New Security Protocols and Patent Filings for Explyra ERP

MB
Mitanshu Bhasin Published January 15, 2026

Silicon Valley, CA — In a move to set a new benchmark for enterprise data protection, Explyra has filed multiple design and utility patents with the USPTO for its groundbreaking "Zero-Knowledge Workspace Architecture."

The patents (including US-2026-EX-0081-A1 and US-2026-EX-0092-B2) cover the proprietary method of integrating AI agents with encrypted data streams without exposing plain-text sensitive information. This ensures that even as the AI learns user patterns, the underlying data remains strictly inaccessible to anyone but the user.

Quantum-Resistant Encryption

As part of the new security rollout, Explyra is transition its entire ecosystem to quantum-resistant encryption protocols. This proactive measure ensures that data stored within Explyra Expense, CRM, and ERP remains secure against future computing threats.

"Security shouldn't be a feature; it should be the foundation," says Mitanshu Bhasin, Founder of Explyra. "Our patent filings represent years of architectural innovation aimed at creating a truly private productivity suite."

Enhanced HIPAA and GDPR Compliance

The new protocols have already received preliminary validation for enhanced HIPAA and GDPR compliance. Large-scale enterprises in the healthcare and finance sectors can now leverage Explyra's Unified SaaS Ecosystem with the confidence of bank-grade security certifications.

The security update will be rolled out automatically to all Explyra users over the coming weeks, requiring no manual configuration. This seamless integration of high-level security is a cornerstone of the Explyra "Zero-Effort" philosophy.
