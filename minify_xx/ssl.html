What is SSL?

SSL (Secure Sockets Layer) is a standard security technology for establishing an encrypted link between a server and a client—typically a web server (website) and a browser, or a mail server and a mail client.

Our Encryption Technology

Explyra uses high-grade 256-bit encryption to secure all data sent to and from our website. This ensures that any information, such as login credentials or financial data, is encrypted and cannot be intercepted by third parties.

  • SHA-256 Fingerprints: Verified certificates by leading Certificate Authorities.
  • HSTS Enabled: HTTP Strict Transport Security (HSTS) ensures your browser always connects via a secure HTTPS link.
  • Regular Security Audits: We periodically audit our SSL configuration to protect against known vulnerabilities.

How to Verify Our Security

You can verify that Explyra is secure by looking for the padlock icon in your browser's address bar. This icon indicates that our SSL certificate is valid and that your connection is fully encrypted.

Infrastructure Security

Beyond SSL, Explyra is built on Google Cloud Platform and Firebase, leveraging their world-class infrastructure and advanced firewall protections to guard against DDoS attacks and unauthorized access attempts.
