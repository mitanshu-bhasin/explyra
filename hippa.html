HIPAA Compliance

Secure Enterprise Health Data Management.

Overview

The Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act (HIPAA) sets the standard for sensitive patient data protection. Companies that deal with protected health information (PHI) must have physical, network, and process security measures in place and follow them to ensure HIPAA compliance.

Our Security Safeguards

Explyra implements the following safeguards to protect health data:

  • Administrative Safeguards: We conduct regular risk assessments and have strictly controlled access to PHI.
  • Physical Safeguards: Our cloud infrastructure providers maintain world-class data center security with 24/7 surveillance and bio-metric access.
  • Technical Safeguards: We use advanced encryption for data at rest and in transit.

Encryption Standards

To ensure PHI is unreadable, undecipherable, and unusable to unauthorized individuals, we employ:

  • AES-256 Encryption: For all data stored in our databases (at rest).
  • TLS 1.3: For all data transmitted between our servers and your devices (in transit).
  • End-to-End Encryption: Available for sensitive health-related notes within the Health Companion suite.

Business Associate Agreements (BAA)

Explyra is ready to sign Business Associate Agreements with our enterprise clients to ensure a shared commitment to PHI protection. Contact our compliance team for more information.

Audit Logging

Every access to sensitive health data is logged and monitored. We maintain detailed audit trails that record who accessed the data, when it was accessed, and what actions were performed.
